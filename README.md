brasagovernment.net — The Government Surface

The Operational Substrate — every government function, mapped, and owned by the citizens of the world.

Part of the BRASA federation. This is the citizen's single map to the entire state: every utility, ministry, autonomous institution, tax, permit, fee, and payment channel — photographed, organized, searchable, and free to use.


What this is

Most people never see the whole state. They see the one office they need this week, found through a search engine, a rumor, or a queue. The government surface exists to replace that with a single, legible map: open the page and the entire apparatus of government is in front of you — as recognizable tiles, not a wall of links — and you are two clicks from the exact agency service you came for.

It is not a portal the government runs. It is a map the citizens own. It advertises nothing, and holds no one's data. Its only job is to make the state findable.

The first instance maps Costa Rica in full. The pattern — Governments, World Wide — is built to generalize to any state.

What it maps

The catalog is comprehensive by design. At present it carries 27 categories spanning roughly 1,365 individual services, organized into two bands:

Utilities (6). The bills every household pays — electricity, water & sewerage, LP gas & fuel, phone, internet & cable, and waste & sanitation (the last enumerated down to per-canton collection across all 84 cantons).

Government of Costa Rica (21). The whole executive and beyond:


The 18 ministries, each opening onto its directorates, attached bodies, and programs — e.g. Hacienda (taxes, customs, the treasury, and the financial sector), Ambiente y Energía (SETENA, SINAC, FONAFIFO, water, energy, mines, meteorology), Salud, Educación, Trabajo, Agricultura, Transportes, and the rest.
Cross-cutting starting points — Presidencia, ICODER, Arranque de Negocios (the business-formalization on-ramp).
Otros Entes Esenciales — the bodies that sit outside the cabinet: the electoral tribunal (TSE), the comptroller (CGR), the ombudsman (Defensoría), the Legislative Assembly, the Judiciary, the professional colleges, and the autonomous public universities.


Every entry resolves to an official source. Nothing is invented; nothing is editorialized.
